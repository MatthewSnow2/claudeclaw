# Skool AI Community: Agentic SCM Presentation Guide

**Date:** 2026-03-01
**Format:** 5 min pitch + 10 min discussion
**Goal:** Market validation, pricing feedback, distributor connections

---

## Opening Hook

"Every supply chain in the world runs on the same system: a human staring at a spreadsheet, guessing how much to order. What if you replaced the guessing with an AI agent that watches inventory in real-time and just... handles it?"

---

## 3-Minute Elevator Pitch (Conversational)

Talk track -- not a script to read verbatim. Hit these beats naturally:

**Beat 1: The Problem (30 sec)**

"So here's the thing about supply chains. The Fortune 500 companies have SAP, Oracle, multi-million dollar systems. But the companies that actually move most of the goods in this country -- regional distributors, wholesalers, the $10M to $100M companies -- they're running on QuickBooks and Excel. They over-order to avoid stockouts, they sit on dead inventory they can't move, and every purchase order is a manual email chain. It's 2026 and they're still faxing POs."

**Beat 2: What Agentic SCM Does (60 sec)**

"We're building AI agents that manage the supply chain autonomously. Not a dashboard you stare at. Not a forecasting model that spits out a report. Actual agents that watch your inventory levels in real-time, detect when you're about to run out of something, generate the purchase order, pick the right supplier, and send it -- all within guardrails you set. Spend limits, vendor whitelists, escalation rules for anything unusual.

Think of it as hiring a supply chain analyst who works 24/7, never sleeps, and gets smarter every cycle. Except it costs $500 a month instead of $80K a year."

**Beat 3: Why We Can Build This (45 sec)**

"We already built the architecture. We have a working simulation that uses the same agent patterns -- signal ingestion, forecasting, procurement, rebalancing -- running on a factory optimization game. 101 tests, 88% code coverage. The game mechanics map almost 1:1 to real supply chain operations. We're not starting from scratch. We're porting proven patterns to a new domain.

The market is $36 billion and growing at 15% a year. McKinsey says AI can cut distributor inventory 20-30%. The enterprise tools can't serve the mid-market because they take 18 months to implement and cost six figures. We can deploy in days."

**Beat 4: Where We Are (30 sec)**

"Right now we have the simulation POC and the pitch. Next step is talking to actual distributors to validate the pain and pricing, then building a beer-game-style demo where you can watch AI agents outperform humans at managing a supply chain. After that, shadow mode with a real distributor -- our agents run alongside their actual operations and we track whether our recommendations would have been better than their actual decisions."

**Transition to discussion:** "That's the thesis. I want to pressure-test it with this group. Where does this break down?"

---

## Discussion Prompts

Use these to steer the conversation. Don't fire them all at once -- pick based on energy in the room.

### Pain Validation
1. "Who here has dealt with stockouts or overstock in a business? What did that actually cost you -- not the textbook answer, the real pain?"
2. "If you run any kind of inventory, what's your reorder process today? How much of it is gut feel vs. data?"

### Market & Positioning
3. "What would you automate first in a supply chain if you could snap your fingers? Purchasing? Forecasting? Warehouse management?"
4. "Do you think mid-market distributors would trust AI to generate purchase orders? What would it take to get them over that trust hurdle?"

### Pricing & Business Model
5. "How would you price an AI-powered SCM tool for a $20M distributor? Per month flat fee? Percentage of savings? Per SKU?"
6. "Would a 'shadow mode' -- where the AI runs alongside your decisions but doesn't act -- be enough to get someone to trial this? Or is that too passive?"

### Technical & Competitive
7. "The big players (SAP, Oracle, Kinaxis) are all talking about AI in supply chain. What stops them from just building this feature? Or does that actually validate the market?"

---

## What Feedback Matthew Is Seeking

Be explicit about this with the group. Tell them:

**"I'm not looking for encouragement. I'm looking for three things:"**

1. **Pain validation** -- Is the mid-market distributor pain real and acute enough to pay for? Or are they happy enough with spreadsheets?
2. **Pricing signal** -- $500/mo for a starter tier, $2K/mo for pro. Does that land? Too cheap (signals low value)? Too expensive (budget doesn't exist)?
3. **Objection mapping** -- What will prospects say no to? Trust issues with AI purchasing? "We already have NetSuite"? "My nephew built me a spreadsheet"?

---

## Specific Asks

Drop these naturally during the conversation, not as a formal list:

- "Know any mid-market distributors I could shadow for a week? I want to sit next to the person who does purchasing and watch what their day actually looks like."
- "Anyone here connected to wholesale distribution trade associations or groups? Industry events where these buyers hang out?"
- "If you know someone running a 3PL or distribution center who'd take a 15-minute call, I'd owe you one."
- "Anyone here built or sold to this segment before? I'd love a 1:1 after this to pick your brain on go-to-market."

---

## Follow-Up Plan

### During the Session
- Take notes on every objection and question. These are gold.
- Note who shows energy on the topic -- those are follow-up 1:1 candidates.
- If someone says "I know a distributor," get the intro committed before the session ends. "Can you text them right now?" works better than "Send me their info later."

### Within 24 Hours
- Post a thank-you in the Skool community thread with a summary of what you heard.
- DM anyone who offered connections or showed interest.
- Update the pitch based on feedback:
  - If pricing got pushback, adjust the value prop framing (lead with ROI, not features).
  - If trust was the main objection, elevate the guardrails/shadow-mode messaging.
  - If nobody cared about the DSP demo, cut it from the pitch and lead with pain/ROI.

### Within 1 Week
- Log all feedback into a structured format (objections, pricing reactions, connection offers).
- Score the beachhead hypothesis: did the Skool group confirm or challenge the SMB distributor thesis?
- If feedback suggests a different beachhead (3PL, manufacturing, services), document the pivot rationale.
- Schedule any distributor calls that came from introductions.

### Decision Gate
After the Skool session, answer this: "Did I learn anything that changes the beachhead, the pricing, or the go-to-market?" If yes, update the strategy docs before the next pitch. If no, proceed to customer discovery interviews with distributors.

## Time Budget

| Block | Duration | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Opening hook + pitch | 5 min | Don't rush. Conversational, not slides. |
| Open discussion | 7-8 min | Let the room drive. Use prompts only if energy drops. |
| Specific asks | 2-3 min | Connections, intros, follow-ups. |
| **Total** | **~15 min** | Stay under. Respect the group's time. |

If the deck is on screen, use it as a visual backdrop only. Do not click through slides while talking. The conversation is the product here, not the presentation.
